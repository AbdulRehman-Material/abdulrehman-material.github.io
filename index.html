About Me

I am a Materials Engineer with research and industrial experience in materials characterization, hydrometallurgy, manufacturing, and quality engineering. My work combines laboratory research with industrial quality control, focusing on sustainable metal extraction, manufacturing processes, materials inspection, and standards compliance. I am particularly interested in developing environmentally sustainable technologies for critical mineral recovery and advanced materials processing.

Selected Research Experience

Glass Fiber Reinforced Concrete (GFRC)

Undergraduate Final Year Project focused on optimizing silica-fume modified glass fiber reinforced concrete through mechanical characterization and processing-property relationships.

Sustainable Zinc Recovery

Research on environmentally sustainable zinc extraction from industrial waste using hydrometallurgical techniques and Deep Eutectic Solvents.

LM13 Aluminium Alloy Casting

Casting, machining and characterization of LM13 aluminium alloy with mechanical testing based on ASTM standards.

Finite Element Simulation

Structural simulation of automotive leaf spring using ANSYS to evaluate stress distribution and deformation behaviour.

Mineral Processing Research

Ore characterization, wet chemical analysis and Atomic Absorption Spectroscopy (AAS) at PCSIR Laboratories.

Quality Engineering

Industrial quality control of gas distribution materials including PE fittings, steel pipelines and manufacturing inspection according to ASTM, API, ASME and ISO standards.
